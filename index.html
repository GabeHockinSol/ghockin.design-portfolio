Planning

When reading the brief, I started to break down each section and take notes so I would gain a clear idea of what the outcome should look like. I also looked at the grading system for this task and took it into consideration to help me understand and evaluate how my portfolio should look.

Throughout the brief it states that planning is a necessary element for this task. I decided to make a flow chart diagram using “Lucid Chart” to help me manage each component to the project. Although this is not an industry favoured way of planning a project compared to other methodologies such as Agile development, I personally felt that this would help me plan and keep track of my work.

Logo Research

When researching logos, I initially looked at big companies such as Apple, Google, Facebook, etc and tried to break down what made their designs iconic. From what I discovered the key rules a lot of successful company’s use is that a logo should have properties of scalability, simplicity, and a simplistic colour scheme. I studied several articles however an article by (Inkbot Design, 2019) came up with a lot of very key points. In this specific article about logo design they go on to explain how a logo is vital part to a business and how colour, creativity and curiosity is crucial to a logo. When discussing Apple’s logo (Inkbot, Design 2019) “Us humans love ourselves a little mystery, and if you can get people to look at your logo for longer, you capture their attention, and it will linger in their memory.” This quote helped me understand how companies like Apple changed from their original logo design to something simpler and more mysteries to capture people’s attention and draw them into their business.

(Inkbot Design, 2019) Draws attention to Coca Cola’s logo and expresses how colour is used. “A strong logo works in black and white too, for instance when it’s reproduced in monochrome for print”. This statement confidently explains that when designing a logo a designer must consider every aspect of how the logo will be displayed. I found this information extremely necessary for when planning my logo and visual identity style guide.

I also studied logo designs from other markets such as music artists. Bands such as “Nirvana” and “The Rolling Stones” brand themselves using key design rules that I previously researched on (Scalability, Simplicity, Creativity, Curiosity and Simplistic colour scheme).

Logo Design

When designing my logo, I did multiple rough sketches of my initials to get an understanding of how my design may look. The theme I chose for my portfolio was minimalism as I personally like that style and felt a less complex design and basic colour scheme would be eye catching and professional in design.

Since my portfolio is about branding myself as a web developer, I researched digital designer/ web developer logos and websites to get an understanding of how they went around their design. A good example was the programming language Python and how the company (Python Software Foundation, 2020) used two snakes as their logo to represent not only the name of their software but also a unique way to market their product as most programming languages are very bland in design.

Unfortunately, when designing my logo an issue I came across was that Programmers/ Web developers as an induvial do not normally brand themselves with logos which made it challenging for me to design my own logo. However, when researching I found a company called Algorithm that specialise in Search Engine Optimisation and their logo/ website design felt unique to the company.

Although a key point of logo design is to add mystery to draw consumers in I personally felt that adding a more programming style to my design would help people understand what it is I do and not look generic.

Visual Identity Style Guide

When designing my portfolio I needed to keep a theme and as discussed previously I would like to keep a minimalist and programming theme to help keep my work easily consistent in design. I will also be considering about publishing my work to social media and how important it is to keep a theme and iconic style to my work.

A Visual Identity Style Guide (VISG) is crucial to almost every design project as it helps keep the design workflow and theme of the project consistent. It is especially important when working in teams and helps document how the project should look.

Before designing my Visual Identity Style Guide, I first studied multiple companies’ approaches to their designs such as Spotify, The London Underground and Netflix. Netflix’s Visual Style Guide has a unique format which is something I have taken inspiration from. For example, Netflix highlights multiple rules in where the company logo can and cannot be displayed. They also state the colour scheme and clearing space which helps promote the logo so that it always stands out and reminds eye catching to the audience.

When making my VISG I broke it down into sections and thought about what I will need for my portfolio. A simple example of this is to cover Typography, Colour Scheme, Clearing Space, Logo Design, Text example and what to avoid. With these core elements put in place it would help me keep my portfolio and future work consistent in design.

After designing an overall template of my VISG I then imported it in to photoshop and created a digital copy. This helped me understand how my theme would look on a digital device and had extra advantages such as adding colour scheme properties next to my colours and adding notes to certain areas such as, Typography uses the font (“Sakbunderan” 1001 fonts, 2019). I also added a few rules such as only using the font in a regular format and not Italics or Bold as this made the design of the font not as appealing. By adding these rules to my VISG it helps keep my design workflow consistent and efficient.

Business Card Design

When designing my Business card, I had to keep my layout of the card scalable and following the brief I made all my designs 85mm x 55mm. I researched various layouts. Since my theme is minimalistic, I needed to focus on keeping my design simple. When researching what goes on a business card, I discovered that a lot designs can be to complex and hard to read. They also focus on sharing the persons contact information as the most important thing on the card. Following the style set out by my VISG I proceeded to design my business card. In the brief it states that my design must be 85mm x 55mm which I took into consideration when in development. I also studied trending business themes and how the designs have changed over time.

An example of minimalistic design which I studied was from an article by (“Hoban Cards, 2019”) in which they discuss the curious nature of business cards used in the film “American Psycho”. (“Hoban Cards, 2019”) “The layout has a professional, familiar feel to it and is well suited in a variety of industries.” This simplistic yet efficient design helped me consider different options and add a more modern theme to my design by incorporating elements of styles used in past decades.

In my final design of my business card I wanted to incorporate other skills I am qualified as, in an attempted to draw in more possible clients. Therefore, I added the job title of “Web/ Games developer”. On the back of the card displays my logo and my contact information. I have added my social media accounts to modernise and adapt my business card to gain a larger following by making it easier to keep in contact with clients.

Final Logo Design

In my design I try and incorporate a simplistic and basic colour scheme to make the logo professional and not complex. In the design I use multiple symbols to represent the fundamentals of my work. For example, the greater/less than and forward dash symbols represent programming. The underscore represents the client I would be working with. My reasoning behind this was to add a level of depth to my creativity and make it seem like the underscore was implying that this should be you i.e. the client. Furthermore, this is the reasoning for having my initials before the forward dash, so the logo is essentially reading “Programmer Gabe Hockin working with Client”. I also changed the font from my initial design to give it a more algorithmic design, once again suggesting a programmer job title.

Portfolio Web Page

When designing my portfolio page for git hub and Adobe Portfolio I took inspiration from already trending companies/ websites such as Apple as they have a similar style to what my theme is. Apple’s layout of their website is clearly on purpose and is clever in how they promote their products by making them the forefront of the website and trying to sell their products straight away. This design philosophy works perfectly with my portfolio as my social media and website should be showcasing my work and try to draw in consumers.

I also researched Adobe Portfolio themes and was drawn in by the “Ludwig Theme” for its simplistic design. When testing the theme, I was intrigued by how the page was laid out and started to sketch out rough ideas of how I could present my work. I was also inspired by the navigation of the website and how it was simple to access different pages and linked social media.

By using buttons that the user can press to navigate up and down the website makes navigation simple, efficient and scale correctly when using different devices. At the bottom of my Git hub page I also link my Adobe Portfolio page for people to follow as well as a social media menu to promote my work.

When designing my web pages, I thought about how to display my work and how it would scale on different devices. In my designs I drew a layout of the fundamentals of where each function should be. For example, the title, header, footer, work, social media and how transitions between my git hub page and Adobe Portfolio page would perform. As a result of this I looked at html5 themes and took inspiration from how people can navigate around a website.

Conclusion

Conclusion In conclusion I have learned a lot about logo design and the importance of how to brand myself. Learning to use visual identity style guides has helped me a lot to keep a theme throughout my work and helped me understand how a colour scheme and scaling is a crucial part of design. I also learned the importance of how planning my work thoroughly before starting development as this helped me keep track of my work. Although I may have encountered some issues along the way such as the font style on Adobe Portfolio forcing me to use a certain type of font, I learned to comprise my design on and make the website fit the visual aesthetic. Going forward I plan to keep building on my Portfolio page and design a blog for my university/ personal work, so I can build a following on social media and keep track of my work.
